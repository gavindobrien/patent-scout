# Patentability Screening Memo: Self-Locking Collapsible Bicycle Pedal with Detent-Confirmed Hinge and Localized-Reinforcement Shell

**Date:** 2026-07-10
**Status:** Preliminary automated screen — not legal advice

## Invention Summary

The invention is a collapsible commuter-bike pedal that folds flat against the crank arm via a spring-loaded ratchet hinge which auto-locks at full (90°) deployment and requires a deliberate twist-and-release motion to collapse, resisting accidental folding under normal pedaling loads. Lock engagement is confirmed by a captive detent ball (integrated into the hinge pin) that seats into a machined groove, giving tactile and audible feedback. The pedal body itself is a two-piece aluminum shell with a steel reinforcement plate localized under the ball-of-foot contact zone, trading full-solid-body strength for reduced weight.

## Element-by-Element Analysis

**Novelty read:** Possibly Anticipated. The general genus (spring/ratchet hinge that self-locks at full deployment without a manual latch) is well established in adjacent folding-product fields; applying it specifically to a pedal-to-crank 90° hinge is less directly documented but is a fairly incremental step from known art.

### 2. Twist-and-release actuation for deliberate unlocking
**What it is:** Fold-down requires a combined rotational-then-axial "twist-and-release" input to disengage the lock, distinct from a push-button or straight-pull release.

**Prior art found:**

- US5388853A — "Lock Mechanism for a Collapsible Stroller": release requires sequential movement in two different orientations, explicitly to demand "calculated effort" and prevent accidental release.
- US9394735B2: requires a two-step rotational sequence (rotate to dislodge pin, then rotate again) before folding is possible — conceptually parallel combined-motion release.
- US5542151A — "Rotatable Bending Joint for Collapsible Playpen": requires an additional lifting motion beyond fold rotation to permit folding.

**Novelty read:** Likely Anticipated. US3303720A discloses essentially the same twist-then-axial-pull release applied directly to folding bicycle pedals nearly 60 years ago — a strong, on-point reference. The broader multi-step deliberate-unlock strategy is also common across strollers, playpens, and furniture hinges.
